Shareholder Perk Calculator
ℹ️ How this works

This tool helps you figure out whether a shareholder membership is worth the money. You list all the perks (benefits) included in the membership, estimate how much each one is worth and how often it gets used, and the calculator tells you whether the total value exceeds what you're paying.

The final score is called the SVS (Shareholder Value Score). An SVS above 1.0 means you're getting more value than you're paying — anything above 1.5 is exceptional.

Step 3 — Your SVS Result
SVS Score (Total Value ÷ Monthly Cost)
—
SVS = Total Monthly Perk Value ÷ Monthly Cost
Below 1.0 — Weak: You're paying more than you're getting back in perks.
1.0–1.2 — Balanced: Value roughly equals cost. Fair deal.
1.2–1.5 — Strong: You're getting noticeably more than you pay.
Above 1.5 — Overpowered: Exceptional value. Perks far exceed cost.

ℹ️ How this works

This side now treats the deal as one fixed contract, not a monthly payout. You enter the total contract cost and the estimated value IB receives from brand, exposure, access, or strategic upside.

IB ROI Score — Is IB getting good value out of the contract? Higher is better. Above 1.0 means the contract value exceeds the contract cost.

Fairness Score — Do the shareholding perks given to the partner stay reasonably balanced against the value IB receives from the contract? A score near 1.0 is the fairest zone for both sides.

Calculated Scores
IB ROI Score ?Measures whether IB is getting good value from the fixed contract. IB ROI = Total Contract Value to IB ÷ Contract Cost to IB.
Is IB getting enough contract value for what it's spending?
—
IB ROI = Total Contract Value to IB ÷ Contract Cost
Below 1.0 — Bad Deal: Costs more than it returns.
1.0–1.3 — Fair: Breaking even or slightly positive.
1.3–2.0 — Good: Clear positive return for IB.
2.0+ — Excellent: Outstanding ROI.
Fairness Score ?Measures whether the shareholding perks given to the partner are balanced against the total contract value IB receives. Fairness Score = Partner Perk ROI ÷ Total Contract Value to IB.
Are the shareholding perks balanced fairly for both sides?
—
Fairness = Partner Perk ROI ÷ Total Contract Value to IB
Below 0.85 — Partner Light: The partner is receiving too little relative to the value they bring.
0.85–1.15 — Balanced: The exchange is in a fair range for both sides.
Above 1.15 — IB Heavy Give: IB may be giving more perk value than it is getting back.
